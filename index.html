How to Play Nine Men's Morris

Phase 1: Placing Pieces

  • Each player takes turns placing one of their nine pieces on an empty spot on the board.
  • If a player places three of their pieces in a straight line (a "mill"), they can remove one of their opponent's pieces.
  • Pieces in a mill cannot be removed unless all of the opponent's pieces are in mills.

Phase 2: Moving Pieces

  • Once all 18 pieces are placed, players take turns moving one of their pieces to an adjacent empty spot along a line.
  • If a player forms a mill, they can remove an opponent's piece as in Phase 1.

Phase 3: Flying Pieces (Optional/Advanced Rule)

  • If a player is reduced to exactly three pieces, they gain the ability to "fly" their pieces.
  • During their turn, they can move any of their pieces to any empty spot on the board (not just adjacent ones).
  • Mills still apply as before.

Winning the Game:

  • A player wins if their opponent is reduced to fewer than three pieces.
  • A player also wins if their opponent has no legal moves.
